--- layout: post title: Riverfront Development date: '2006-02-22T14:43:00.000-05:00' author: DA tags: modified_time: '2006-02-22T15:42:15.210-05:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-17447662.post-114064093517267524 blogger_orig_url: http://daveande.blogspot.com/2006/02/riverfront-development.html --- Over the last month there's been a lot of exciting news about Detroit's riverfront. Two of the three former cement silos are now slated for redevelopment that will include condos, retail, and restaurants, creating a walkable/liveable community along the river. This announcement comes just a month after GM selected a development firm for property just east of the Ren Cen, and construction is set to begin on the new Asian Village development in the same area. It looks like this neighborhood is well on it's way to taking off and becoming a downtown destination. I wish it could happen overnight.

Detroit's Super Bowl success, riverfront news, and news that the city leads southeast Michigan in new housing permits should finally give some skeptical suburbanites legitimate reasons to get excited about their city.
